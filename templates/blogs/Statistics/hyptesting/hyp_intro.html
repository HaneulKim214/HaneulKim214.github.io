What is Hypothesis Testing?

For a while I had hard time understanding what exactly Hypothesis testing is since there are too many similar statistical jargons. Here I will go through an example question and explain which part I was confused and explain differences between two jargons that might be confusing to most people like me.

Here are some important jargons in understanding what hypothesis testing is:

  • Null hypothesis = Initial belief in your experiment.
  • Alternative hypothesis = New belief that you want to verify by rejecting null hypothesis.
  • Z-score = How many standard deviation away from the mean is this value you want to test.
  • P-value = probability of such event given null hypothesis is true.
  • Type I error = Rejecting null when it is true. Also known as False positive
  • alpha = significance level = probabilty of Type I error occuring.
  • Type II error = Accepting null hypothesis when it is false. Known as False negative

Hypothesis testing is used to test whether your result your experiment produced means something. For example if you invented a drug for covid-19 and want to see if it actually cures people with the virus we would use hypothesis testing to see if it is the drug that cures people with the virus instead of something else we do not know.

Before we move on let me claify some jargons. I was very confused with p-value, type I error, alpha, and significance level. If you want to test effectiveness of your drug you wouldn't try in on all population since it would be expensive therefore select sample from the population and experiment. So with the outcome of the sample we have to conclude whether our intial belief (drug is effective) is true or reject intial belief (drug is ineffective).

Probability of getting outcome you've got from the sample assuming intial belief is true is called a p-value. Low p-value implies that assuming inital belief outcome is very unlikely to happen thus we would reject intial belief. p-value = 0.05 => 5% chance outcome is likely to happen if initial belief is true which is very unlikely. Alpha and significance level basically mean the same thing, it says that how low of percentage will you give to reject initial belief. So alpha = 0.05 means that if p-value is less than 0.05 we will reject initial belief and if alpha = 0.01 it means that p-value has to be less than 0.01 to be able to reject inital belief.

Alpha and significance level is basically threshold for deciding whether you can reject initial belief. In other words it is how much risk are you willing to take, that is how much Type I error is acceptable? If you are building a recommendation system you would set alpha to be 0.05 or higher since giving incorrect recommendation is not crucial to living, in other words it is acceptible to have Type I error.

But why use hypothesis testing when we could just look at the outcome of experiment and make a conclusion?


Why Hypothesis Testing?

It is a way of making conclusion with reduced subjectivity therefore less prone to bias.
Let me show you why using an example.

You are running candy bar factory and on average their weight is 5g. New employee recruited today comes into your office and tells you that average weight of candy bars are not 5g.

Your initial belief is that it is 5g, this is called a Null hypothesis. We assume this is true untill new accusation is made. This new accusation from the new employee is called an Alternative hypothesis. We assume our initial belief is true since we've been running candy factory for a while and if new employee provides enough evidence that his/her accusation is true then we reject our initial belief and accept new belief that average weight of candy bars are not 5g.

So to test this we would select 50 candy bars at random and calculate average weight. Say you do this 3 times and outputs averages of (5.02g, 5.65g, and 7.22g). For first outcome most people will agree that our null is true since 5.02g is pretty close to 5g. Our last outcome 7.22g, most people will agree with alternative hypothessis. What about when sample outputted average weight as 5.65g? Some might agree on null hypothesis and some might not => subjective.

To reduce subjectivity in our conclusion we use hypothesis testing.

Inferential Statistics

In study of statistics there are two main branches which are Descriptive statistics and Inferential statistics.

Descriptive Statistics focuses on collecting, summarizing, and presenting set of data. For example, given set of data descriptive statistics calculate its mean, median, mode, standard deviation and so on to describe given set. These descriptions can be visualized using graphs and charts.

Inferential Statistics uses a subset of population called sample to make inference about the whole population. For example, if you want to find mean height for all males in Asia it would be very hard and time consuming to measure each and every height of male in Asia therefore we would sample 1000 people and calculate their mean height and use calculated mean height of sample to infer about mean height of whole population. Note that 1000 sample must be representative of the entire population.
